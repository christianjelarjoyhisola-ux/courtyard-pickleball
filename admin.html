PLATFORM ACCESS & BOOKING FEE AUTHORIZATION AGREEMENT
CourtYard Pickleball Booking System — Version 2 · Fixed Terms

Read the full agreement before signing. The Sign button unlocks only after you scroll to the bottom.

1. Parties

Platform Provider: Christian Jelar Joy Hisola ("Platform Provider"), the sole developer and owner of the CourtYard Pickleball Booking System, a software platform that enables customers to discover and book pickleball courts online.

Court Owner: The individual or business identified by the account below ("Court Owner"), who independently owns, operates, and is solely responsible for the physical CourtYard Pickleball court in Sibagat, Agusan del Sur.

The Platform Provider has no involvement in the physical operations, staffing, pricing decisions, customer disputes, or revenues of the court. The Platform Provider provides only the online booking software as a service.

2. What the Platform Provides

The Platform Provider grants the Court Owner access to the following at no monthly subscription cost to the Court Owner:

  • Online court booking page accessible to the public
  • Real-time slot availability management
  • Automated GCash receipt fraud detection and verification
  • Admin dashboard for managing bookings, courts, and staff accounts
  • Automated customer notifications and booking references
  • Platform hosting, security, and technical maintenance

3. Platform Booking Fee & Weekly Remittance

In exchange for free access to the platform, the Court Owner authorizes the Platform Provider to add a non-refundable platform booking fee of ₱15.00 (fifteen pesos, fixed) to each customer transaction processed through the system. This fee is charged to and paid by the customer/player, not the Court Owner — similar to how Airbnb, Grab, or Booking.com charge a service fee to the end user.

The ₱15.00 platform fee is fixed and permanent. It will not increase, decrease, or change for any reason, and no other fees will be charged to the Court Owner or customers beyond those the Court Owner sets for their own court rates.

Billable event. The ₱15.00 fee applies to each confirmed booking made through the platform (a booking that successfully reserved a court slot). Bookings that are cancelled before confirmation, rejected during payment verification, or that never held a slot are not billed.

Collection. The Court Owner collects the ₱15.00 platform fee from the customer together with the court rate at the time of booking, and holds it on behalf of the Platform Provider until remittance.

Weekly remittance. Billing is settled weekly. Each week the Platform Provider issues a statement covering every confirmed booking from the previous Monday–Sunday period. The amount due equals the number of confirmed bookings in that week × ₱15.00. Each statement itemises the exact bookings included, and each booking is billed only once.

Due date & method. Payment is due within five (5) days of the statement date. The Court Owner remits the amount due via GCash to the platform remittance account shown in the dashboard, then submits the GCash transaction reference and a screenshot of the receipt as proof of payment through the "Pay Now" function.

Non-payment. Statements unpaid past the due date are marked overdue. The Platform Provider may suspend the Court Owner's access to the booking system until all outstanding statements are settled. Suspension does not waive any amounts already due.

4. Court Owner's Revenue

All court booking fees set by the Court Owner (e.g. ₱200/hr, ₱250/hr) belong exclusively to the Court Owner. The Platform Provider does not take any share of the court rental revenue. The Court Owner receives 100% of the court rate from every confirmed booking. Only the separate ₱15.00 platform fee (paid by the customer) goes to the Platform Provider.

5. Anti-Circumvention

The Court Owner agrees not to direct, encourage, or assist customers to bypass the platform in order to avoid the ₱15.00 booking fee. This includes but is not limited to: advertising a direct booking channel at a lower price, accepting bookings verbally or via personal chat for slots visible on the platform, or instructing customers to "book directly" to avoid the fee.

Circumvention of the platform fee is a material breach of this Agreement and may result in immediate termination of platform access.

6. Obligation to Honor Bookings

The Court Owner agrees to honor all confirmed bookings made through the platform. Customers who receive a booking confirmation with a reference number must be allowed access to the court at the reserved time. Cancellations due to the Court Owner's failure (not the customer's) are a breach of this Agreement.

7. Court Owner's Responsibilities

  • Keep court availability, rates, and operating hours accurate on the platform
  • Manage and respond to customer concerns about the physical court experience
  • Ensure the court is safe, operational, and accessible during booked hours
  • Keep admin login credentials confidential and not share access with unauthorized persons
  • Not export or misuse customer data for purposes unrelated to court management

8. Independence of Parties

The Court Owner and Platform Provider are fully independent parties. This Agreement does not create a partnership, franchise, joint venture, employment, or agency relationship. The Platform Provider is not responsible for:

  • Disputes between the Court Owner and customers
  • Physical injuries or accidents at the court
  • Loss of revenue due to court downtime or customer cancellations
  • Any business, tax, or legal obligations of the Court Owner

9. Platform Availability

The Platform Provider will make reasonable efforts to keep the system available. However, the Platform Provider does not guarantee 100% uptime and is not liable for losses caused by temporary platform outages, third-party service interruptions (e.g. Supabase, Cloudflare), or force majeure events.

10. Termination

Either party may terminate this Agreement with 14 days written notice via GCash message or email. The Platform Provider may terminate immediately and without notice if the Court Owner breaches Sections 5 (Anti-Circumvention) or 6 (Honor Bookings), engages in fraud, or misuses customer data.
